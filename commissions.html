I've been playing around with SculptrVR and I think I've practiced enough to the point where I'd like to offer my services! If you're looking for a 3D model made with love from scratch, I'm available.

I'm offering a variety of "tiers" from simple static meshes, sculpted and traditionally-modeled/lowpoly objects, and even fully rigged Tower Unite, NEOS, or VRChat ready models.

Simply select a tier and a product and get in touch with me. I'll try to communicate as openly as I can and send dailies on my progress as I work on your model!

Products

Here are the "products" I'm offering, i.e. the actual thing you will recieve from me:

Snapshot

Starting as low as $1, if all you want is a nice mesh with some decent lighting as seen in SculptrVR I can construct a scene exactly as you'd like it. 4k PNG (usually about 8MB). I don't have the best GPU but I'll try to max out as many settings as I can before my PC crawls to a stutter.

Blender Render

If you want something that looks a bit more professional, we can pop the model into Blender and give it the full treatment: raytraced lighting with Cycles, all new materials from Blenderkit, postprocessing effects, the whole 9 yards.

Raw SculptrVR Output

Exactly as it came out of the app, if you want a static mesh to play with as you please, no restrictions, you can have it. These models will be extremely high poly. They should not be used in video games or metaverse apps like Tower Unite, VRChat, or NEOS. They should be great for 3D printing though.

Decimated Blender Sculpt

The exported mesh can then be imported to Blender and decimated. This isn't just "throwing it in a plugin and letting it do the work": I'll go through with a fine tooth comb and fix any vertex complexity, boolean out the layers so they stick together (except like eyes), and even go through and vertex paint the model again if anything got lost. By your request, I can even convert the vertex painting to UVs so you have traditional textures. These models are perfect for real-time rendering and video games. The textures will be pretty simplistic but they'll get the job done.

Retextured Decimation

...unless you want me to try to make them better.

Full disclaimer: I am not adept at texture painting whatsoever. I was never really good at painting in general. (I'm an illustrator, sorry.) Expect very flat colors and photo-kitbashed textures.

If you're into that though, yeah, I can do it. Better than no textures at all, I guess.

Fully Rigged Mesh

The whole shebang. Shell out and you get it all - a fully rigged, perfectly weight painted model. For humanoid models, I'll try to map it to the Tower Unite skeleton, which is made to mimic the Source SMD skeleton and will be recognized by Unity's Humanoid Avatar system. This means the model can easily be converted for GMod, Source Filmmaker, and VRChat, and it can be directly imported with no changes into Tower Unite and NEOS.

I consider the price point on this tier to be flexible - I'm willing to apply discounts for less complex models, or charge extra for more complexity. For example, a model with no mouth - like a robot or power armor - would need no visemes. $5 off! But on the opposite end, a model with one tail will require changes to the armature to add bones to the hip, but that's usually fine. Once you start getting into multiple tails, the rigging and weight painting complexity increases, and I might charge extra.

Tiers

I'm offering 4 simple "tiers" for what kind of items you can get from me.

Simple Mesh

A basic sculpt, great for simple renders or 3D printing. If you need to print a miniature or want a small sculpture for your Tower Unite condo, this should do.

Inorganic Object/Lowpoly Mesh

Rather than scuplting, I can create a simple mesh for you using traditional modeling methods like extruding and beveling. This is good for furniture or other simple objects.

Single Figure

A fully modeled figure, humanoid or otherwise. Unless specified, humanoid figures will be modeled in a T or star pose. Quadrepedal ones will just be in a basic reference pose.

Full Scene

This is a wild one, but if you want like a full blown sculpted out scene of whatever you like, I can do that too.

License?

After recieving whatever it is you ordered, you're given a full permissive license to transform the object however you like. Retexture it, rerig it, move vertices around, throw on new clothing, put it in Gmod or Source Filmmaker, give a copy to a friend so they can retexture it or even just use it, whatever.

However, I would request that you not resell the product unless you give a similar permissive license and transfer all transformative rights to the person you're reselling to. (I.E. you transfer the model, then remove the model from your digital possession completely.) The reason should be obvious: I ain't making models to be mass-produced here. I'm perfectly capable of doing that myself, and I'd rather not just get cut out of profit for a Gumroad scam or Etsy scheme.

But because the kind of people who commission someone and then turn around and sell the result digitally for a profit aren't the kind of people to uphold license agreements, allow me to dissuade them a different way: If I find out you're mass producing a commission I sold you, I still have the model. I can undersell you. It doesn't matter how cheap you make it. Don't tempt me.
